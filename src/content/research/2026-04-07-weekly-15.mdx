This week made decent progress on the convergence rate proof for the adaptive gradient method.

## What I did

- Filled in the gap in Lemma 3.2 — the key was bounding the variance term using the smoothness constant $L$ more carefully.
- Read [Defazio et al. 2023] on schedule-free optimizers; their variance reduction trick might be applicable.
- Met with advisor Thursday, got feedback to tighten the assumptions in Section 4.

## The key inequality

The main bound now looks like:

$$
\mathbb{E}\left[\|\nabla f(x_t)\|^2\right] \leq \frac{2(f(x_0) - f^*)}{T} + \frac{\sigma^2 L}{T}
$$

where $\sigma^2$ is the gradient noise variance. Still need to handle the non-convex case.
